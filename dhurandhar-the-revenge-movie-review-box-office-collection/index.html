What is Dhurandhar The Revenge All About?

"Dhurandhar: The Revenge" is the thrilling conclusion to the espionage duology, picking up directly after the events of the first film. It plunges deeper into the murky world of undercover operations, where Indian intelligence agent Jaskirat Singh Rangi (Ranveer Singh), operating as Hamza Ali Mazari, continues his perilous mission within Karachi's criminal syndicates and political power structures in Pakistan. The film not only focuses on avenging the 26/11 attacks but also introduces bigger threats that Hamza must confront. The plot, as officially revealed, centers on Hamza Ali Mazari plotting his rise in the gangster-terror-political network of Pakistan, aiming to bring India's enemies to justice following the death of Rehman Dakait.

The Story So Far (and What's Next)

The first "Dhurandhar" film established Hamza Ali Mazari's infiltration of a Karachi-based criminal outfit led by Rehman Dakait, a gangster with significant political ambitions. Hamza, a RAW agent, strategically worked to destabilize the relationship between Dakait and his political mentor, Jameel Jamali. The previous installment concluded with Hamza successfully ambushing Dakait, a pivotal moment that set the stage for his emergence as "Sher-E-Baloch". "Dhurandhar: The Revenge" delves into Hamza's origins while escalating towards a brutal final confrontation with Major Iqbal, the ISI operative who has been a constant thorn in his side. The sequel aims to blur the lines between heroism and monstrosity as Hamza transforms from a patriotic agent into a figure embroiled in a violent personal war.

The Visionary Behind the Lens: The Director

Aditya Dhar, the acclaimed writer, co-producer, and director, returns to helm "Dhurandhar: The Revenge". Known for his impactful storytelling and dynamic action sequences, Dhar has once again delivered a film that is both visually stunning and emotionally resonant. His vision for the "Dhurandhar" universe is evident in every frame, promising a cinematic experience that is both grand and deeply personal. Several critics and industry experts have lauded Dhar as a "true visionary and a mastermind" who gives actors the freedom to perform while ensuring the scene's perfection.

The Star-Studded Cast of Dhurandhar The Revenge

One of the major draws of the "Dhurandhar" franchise has always been its powerful ensemble cast, and "The Revenge" is no exception. The film boasts a lineup of some of the most formidable talents in Indian cinema, each delivering performances that elevate the narrative.

Leading the Charge: Protagonists

Ranveer Singh reprises his role as the enigmatic Hamza Ali Mazari, also known as Jaskirat Singh Rangi. Singh's portrayal of the deep-cover agent is said to be "phenomenal—ferocious, intense, and easily a career-best". He fully owns this spy universe, unleashing a "beasty avatar" that has captivated audiences. His transformation from a patriotic agent to a character consumed by personal revenge is central to the film's emotional weight.

The Forces of Antagonism: Villains

Arjun Rampal returns as the menacing Major Iqbal, an ISI officer and Hamza's primary antagonist. His performance is described as adding "incredible gravitas" to the ensemble, making him a formidable opponent. The clash between Hamza and Major Iqbal is a cornerstone of the film's intense action and dramatic tension.

Supporting Pillars

The film features a stellar supporting cast that includes:

  • Sanjay Dutt as SP Chaudhary Aslam, Sindh Police, who is based on a real-life police officer. Dutt's presence adds a gritty authenticity to the narrative.
  • R. Madhavan as Ajay Sanyal, the Director of IB, believed to be based on India's National Security Advisor, Ajit Doval. Madhavan's role as the strategic mastermind provides crucial depth to the intelligence operations.
  • Sara Arjun as Yalina Jamali, Jameel's daughter and Hamza's wife, a character pivotal to Hamza's undercover life.
  • Rakesh Bedi as Jameel Jamali, a senior politician and Member of the National Assembly of Pakistan.
  • Gaurav Gera as Mohammed Aalam, a juice shop owner and Hamza's handler in Lyari.
  • Danish Pandor as Uzair Baloch, leader of the Baloch gang.
  • Akshaye Khanna appears in flashback scenes as Rehman Dakait, the gang leader from the first film, whose death sparks the events of the sequel.

The collective power of these performances creates a rich tapestry of characters, each contributing significantly to the film's complex narrative.

Unpacking the Themes and Emotional Core

"Dhurandhar: The Revenge" is more than just an action thriller; it delves deep into complex themes, exploring the psychological toll of vengeance, the blurred lines of morality in espionage, and the unwavering spirit of patriotism.

Action, Drama, and Beyond

The film is touted as an "explosive, emotional, and deeply satisfying" experience, with action sequences described as "massive, raw, and goosebump-inducing". However, it's the emotional impact that truly sets it apart. The themes of revenge and moral ambiguity are central, as Hamza Ali Mazari navigates the treacherous underworld, transforming into a figure embroiled in a violent personal war. This journey challenges the audience to question the nature of heroism and the sacrifices made in the pursuit of justice. The violence, while extreme in places, is noted by early viewers as being "justified" within the narrative.

The Music and Cinematography

The background music (BGM) and cinematography have received high praise, with many calling them "top-tier". Shashwat Sachdev, who composed the music, has delivered a pulsating, techno-driven sound that enhances the film's high-energy groove. Vikash Nowlakha is credited for the cinematography, capturing the gritty realism and expansive scale of the film's various locations, including Punjab, Chandigarh, Maharashtra, Ladakh, Himachal Pradesh in India, and Thailand, with some areas doubling for Pakistan-set sequences. These technical aspects play a crucial role in immersing the audience in Hamza's world and intensifying the emotional stakes. The film's second track, "Main Aur Tu," composed by Sachdev and featuring Jasmine Sandlas and Reble, has already garnered significant attention.

Dhurandhar The Revenge Movie Review and its Already Collection and Expected Box Office Collection: Early Numbers and Projections

The initial box office performance of "Dhurandhar: The Revenge" is nothing short of historic. Even before its official worldwide release on March 19, 2026, the film has shattered records, indicating a monumental run at the global box office.

The Opening Weekend Impact

"Dhurandhar: The Revenge" has already made headlines by crossing the Rs 200 crore mark in gross advance sales worldwide, making it the first Bollywood film to achieve this feat. This surpasses records previously held by major Indian films like "Pushpa 2," "Leo," "RRR," and "Baahubali 2" in terms of advance bookings.

Here's a breakdown of its impressive pre-release collection:

  • Worldwide Advances (Opening Weekend): A staggering Rs 187 crore approx.. This number quickly surpassed Rs 200 crore with advances from India and eastern overseas markets.
  • India Advances (Opening Weekend): Exceeded Rs 100 crore.
  • Overseas Advances (Opening Weekend): Approximately USD 9 million.
  • Opening Day Pre-sales: Over Rs 100 crore, including Rs 73 crore from the domestic box office and Rs 35 crore from overseas screens.
  • Paid Previews Collection: Predictions suggested the film would earn approximately Rs 50 crore from its paid previews alone, which commenced on March 18, a day before its official release. If true, this would be a record for paid previews in Bollywood.

The film has also set a new lifetime record on BookMyShow, selling 14.81 lakh tickets until Wednesday, breaking Shah Rukh Khan's "Jawan" record of 11.02 lakh tickets within the same timeframe. It is releasing in over 17,000 shows across multiple languages, a testament to its widespread anticipation.

What the Future Holds: Box Office Predictions

Industry analysts are projecting an unprecedented run for "Dhurandhar: The Revenge." Elara Capital's Karan Taurani expects the film to open at a record-breaking Rs 100 crore net domestic box office on day one across more than 8,000 screens. Furthermore, the film is predicted to achieve net domestic lifetime collections of Rs 1,100–1,300 crore, potentially becoming India's first Hindi original film to cross the Rs 1,000 crore mark. This performance is seen as a crucial boost for the subdued fourth quarter for FY26 at the box office, potentially supporting overall collections and occupancy levels for cinema operators. The combined box office collections of the "Dhurandhar" franchise (parts 1 and 2) may even reach around Rs 2,000 crore, potentially surpassing benchmarks set by "Baahubali" and "Pushpa". This would solidify the "Dhurandhar" franchise as one of the biggest-grossing film properties in India.

Where to Catch Dhurandhar The Revenge

The excitement for "Dhurandhar: The Revenge" is global, and fans are eager to know how and where they can experience this cinematic event.

Theatrical Release

"Dhurandhar: The Revenge" was released in theatres worldwide on March 19, 2026, coinciding with major festivals like Gudi Padwa, Ugadi, and Eid al-Fitr, strategically timed to maximize its reach. Unlike its first part, the sequel is also being released in Telugu, Tamil, Malayalam, and Kannada languages, catering to massive fan demand across India. The film has received an 'A' (adults only) certificate from the CBFC due to strong violence, with a final runtime of 229 minutes (3 hours and 49 minutes). Paid preview shows were held on the evening of March 18, adding to the pre-release fervor. It's important to note that, similar to its predecessor, the film has been banned in Gulf Cooperation Council countries.
